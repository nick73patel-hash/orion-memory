Mobile ADAS (Advanced Driver Assistance Systems) calibration business.

**Partners:** Jason (highly experienced auto body professional)

**Why:** 80%+ of new vehicles have ADAS. Any collision, windshield replacement, or alignment job requires recalibration. Most body shops and glass shops don't have the equipment. Insurance companies now require documented calibration. Partner has existing industry relationships — the hardest part of customer acquisition is already solved.

**How to apply:** When working on this project, lead with the mobile-first model targeting body shops and glass shops as B2B recurring clients. Partner's relationships are the primary competitive advantage.

## Business Model
- **Mobile first** — go to body shops, glass shops, dealerships
- **Fixed location later** — add shop once volume justifies it
- **B2B recurring** — body shops become regular clients, not one-off customers

## Revenue
- Per calibration: $150–$500+ (luxury/EV: $500–$1,000+)
- Target: 6–10 jobs/day per van
- At $250 avg × 8 jobs × 250 days = ~$500K/year gross per van

## Startup Costs
- ADAS calibration equipment: $20,000–$50,000
- Diagnostic software subscriptions: $3,000–$8,000/yr
- Van (if mobile): $35,000–$60,000
- Training and certification: $2,000–$5,000
- Insurance (garage liability + commercial auto): $5,000–$10,000/yr
- Total to launch: ~$60,000–$120,000

## Top Equipment Brands
Hunter Engineering, Autel, Bosch, Snap-on, OPUS IVS

## Key Risks
- OEM restrictions (some brands require dealer-only calibration)
- Rapid tech changes require constant software updates
- Static calibration needs flat, controlled space with 20–30 ft clearance
- Dealerships building in-house capability
