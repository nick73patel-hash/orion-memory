## Product Strategy — Two Line Approach

### Line 1 — Pultruded Fiberglass Windows (Canada)
- **Suppliers:** Accurate Dorwin (Winnipeg, MB) and SILEX (Winnipeg, MB)
- **Material:** True pultruded fiberglass — same category as Marvin Ultrex
- **Tariff:** Duty-free under USMCA
- **Certifications:** AAMA, NFRC, Energy Star — already certified
- **Warranty:** 20 years
- **Price vs. US brands:** 20–30% below Marvin Essential
- **Target market:** High-end residential, custom builders, mountain/resort markets

### Line 2 — Aluminum-Clad Solid Wood Windows (Poland)
- **Suppliers:** Sokółka (sokolka.us) and/or Debesto (debesto.com/en-us)
- **Material:** Solid wood interior, aluminum-clad exterior
- **Tariff:** ~15% (HTS 4418.10 — wood-primary classification, pending binding CBP ruling)
- **Certifications:** CE, Passive House, ift Rosenheim (EU) — AAMA/NFRC obtainable with $30–75K testing investment
- **Warranty:** 5–10 years standard (negotiate enhanced terms for US market)
- **Price vs. US brands:** 30–50% below Andersen 400 / Marvin Elevate (post-tariff)
- **Target market:** Architects, custom builders, luxury residential, passive house projects

## Material Research — Fiberglass Window Hierarchy

### True Pultruded Fiberglass (Premium)
Continuous lineal glass fibers (65–75% by weight) saturated in thermoset resin, pulled through heated die. Cannot melt. Extremely dimensionally stable. Expands/contracts at nearly same rate as glass.

| Brand | Material | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Marvin Ultrex (Essential/Elevate/Infinity) | True pultruded fiberglass | Gold standard. 34,000 psi tensile. AAMA 624/625 finish. 20-yr warranty. |
| Pella Impervia (Duracast) | True pultruded fiberglass | Legitimate but lower-tier. Grainy finish, powder coat, 10-yr warranty only. Often costs MORE than Marvin. |
| Andersen Fibrex | NOT fiberglass | 40% wood fiber + 60% vinyl (thermoplastic). 3x weaker than Ultrex. Misleading marketing. |

### Canadian Fiberglass Manufacturers (Marvin-Quality Alternatives)
All duty-free under USMCA. All NFRC/AAMA certified. All active US distribution.

| Manufacturer | Location | Standout |
|---|---|---|
| **Accurate Dorwin** | Winnipeg, MB | Possibly world's first pultruded fiberglass window (1983). Outstanding builder reputation. |
| **SILEX** | Winnipeg, MB | Most certifications (AAMA 101, NFRC, EPD). Commercial-grade. Active US sales. |
| **Fibertec** | Concord, ON | 30+ years. Strong high-performance builder reputation. |
| **Cascadia** | Langley, BC | ~58% recycled fiberglass content. Best environmental story. PHIUS certified. |
| **Duxton** | Winnipeg, MB | Extreme cold-climate performance. 4.7/5 Houzz. |
| **Inline Fiberglass** | Ontario | PHIUS certified. Watch IGU seal complaint history. |

### Why India, Dubai, and China Don't Work for Fiberglass
- **India:** Chopped strand mat FRP only — not true pultrusion. No US certs. Not comparable quality.
- **Dubai/UAE:** Industrial GRP only (tanks, pipes). No fenestration manufacturers.
- **China (Nanjing/Jiangsu):** Real pultruded profile makers exist (Spare Composites, Shengze) but no finished window brand with AAMA/NFRC and a 20-yr warranty. Tariffs at ~22.8–32.8% stack. Active antidumping investigation on adjacent fiberglass door products (198% alleged margin). 12–18 month development project minimum.
- **Poland:** Strong PVC/aluminum industry but NO pultruded fiberglass manufacturers.

## Material Research — Aluminum-Clad Windows

### Tariff Reality by Country (2025–2026)

| Country | HTS (aluminum) | HTS (wood-primary) | Combined Tariff (aluminum) | Combined Tariff (wood-primary) | Viable? |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Poland/EU** | 7610.10 | **4418.10** | ~65% | **~15%** | **YES** |
| China | 7610.10 | 4418.10 | ~73% | ~25–35% | No |
| UAE | 7610.10 | N/A (no wood-clad) | ~65% | N/A | No |
| Canada | 7610.10 | 4418.10 | Duty-free (USMCA) | Duty-free | Yes (limited supply) |

**Key CBP ruling:** NY K82426 — when structural frame is wood and exterior is metal-clad, essential character is wood → classifies under HTS 4418, not 7610. This eliminates the Section 232 aluminum tariff (50%).

### Polish Aluminum-Clad Wood Window Manufacturers

| Manufacturer | Notes |
|---|---|
| **Sokółka** (sokolka.us) | Part of Inwido AB (Sweden). 50+ years. Active US/Canada export. "Euro Alu" aluminum-clad solid wood line. Best first direct supplier contact. |
| **Debesto** (debesto.com/en-us) | US-facing B2B import broker aggregating Polish manufacturers. Best Phase 1 partner — handles logistics, customs, has US price lists. |
| **Oknoplast** (oknoplast.us) | Active US dealer network. Colorado distributor exists (Window Distributors of Colorado). PIXEL and ALUHAUS aluminum lines. |
| **Drutex** | Exports to 70+ countries including Americas. Massive scale. More supply chain play than consumer brand. |
| **Internorm** | Austrian-HQ, manufactured in Central Europe. KF 410 and HF 310 aluminum-clad wood. Used by high-end US custom builders. |

### Why China and UAE Don't Work for Aluminum-Clad
- **China:** Section 232 aluminum tariff (50%) + Section 301 (7.5%) + MFN (5.7%) = ~73%. Effectively prohibitive. Wood-primary classification possible but risky without binding CBP ruling.
- **UAE:** Same ~65% tariff stack. Aluminum-only products (no wood-clad). Fabricators using European systems, not system developers. No US certifications.

## Strategic Roadmap

### Phase 1 — Validate Demand (0–12 months)
- Source Line 1 (fiberglass) direct from Accurate Dorwin or SILEX — already US-certified and active
- Source Line 2 (aluminum-clad) via Debesto.com — broker handles logistics, low risk
- Target: Colorado mountain/resort market (custom builders, luxury residential)
- Establish LLC, import bonds, freight forwarder relationship

### Phase 2 — Go Direct (12–24 months)
- Negotiate direct distribution agreement with Sokółka (sokolka.us) for Colorado/mountain territory
- Establish exclusive regional territory rights in exchange for volume commitments
- Begin building contractor and architect relationships

### Phase 3 — Certifications (parallel with Phase 2)
- Budget $30–75K for NFRC + AAMA testing on 2–3 core Polish SKUs (casement, fixed picture, sliding patio)
- Energy Star certification follows — unlocks federal tax credits for buyers
- Once certified, can sell into commercial, code-required, and architect-specified projects

### Phase 4 — Legal/Customs
- Obtain binding CBP ruling confirming aluminum-clad solid wood windows = HTS 4418.10 (wood-primary)
- Locks in ~15% tariff rate from Poland
- Work with customs attorney (budget $5–10K)
