# Perpetual Blue — Grenada & Southern Grenadines Itinerary

**Boat:** 2013 Fountaine Pajot Sanya 57 Catamaran — "Perpetual Blue"
**When:** Late August (hurricane season — boat based in Grenada)
**Duration:** 8–9 days

---

## Pre-Trip Notes

**Late August conditions:** South of hurricane belt (~12°N). Lighter trades, warm water (82–84°F), lush green islands, fewer boats than winter season. Afternoon squalls possible — easy to dodge on a cat.

**Distances are short:** Grenada → Carriacou = 30nm. Carriacou → Tobago Cays = 20nm. Morning sails, full afternoons at anchor.

**Customs:** Grenada and Carriacou = same country, no clearance needed. Crossing into St. Vincent & the Grenadines (Tobago Cays, Mayreau, Union Island) = clear in at **Union Island, Clifton Harbour**.

---

## Day-by-Day

### Day 1 — Arrive · Prickly Bay, Grenada
- **Anchorage:** Prickly Bay (L'Anse aux Epines) — well-protected, good holding
- Arrive, check boat systems, provision at Budget Marine / IGA
- Dinner ashore: Nimrod's or La Boulangerie (Grand Anse)

### Day 2 — Grenada Day Sail · Hog Island / Clarks Court Bay
- **Distance:** 5nm
- Sail around Grenada's dramatic south coast
- Anchor at Hog Island — calm, turquoise, protected
- Sundowners at Turtleback Bar (floating bar on converted boat)

### Day 3 — Carriacou · Tyrrel Bay
- **Distance:** 30nm north · 4–5 hour sail
- One of the Caribbean's great anchorages — wide, protected, clear water
- Walk into Hillsborough, explore traditional wooden boat building on the beach
- Dinner: Lazy Turtle or Bogles Round House (book ahead)

### Day 4 — Sandy Island · Best Beach Day
- **Distance:** 3nm off Carriacou
- White sand spit, coconut palms, zero development — top 5 beaches in the Caribbean
- Anchor in 8–10ft right off the beach
- Paddleboards, snorkel gear, all day
- Back to Tyrrel Bay for the night

### Day 5 — Tobago Cays · Crown Jewel
- **Distance:** 20nm · 3 hour sail
- Clear into SVG at Union Island, Clifton Harbour first
- Anchor in the Horseshoe Reef — completely flat water
- Swimming with sea turtles (they're everywhere)
- World-class snorkeling — 60–80ft visibility
- Sunset from the beach on Baradal Island

### Day 6 — Tobago Cays · Stay Another Day
- Dinghy to **Petit Tabac** — tiny uninhabited island, Pirates of the Caribbean filming location
- Afternoon snorkel south side of reef — bigger fish, different terrain
- Buy fresh lobster from local fishermen who come to the boat every morning

### Day 7 — Mayreau · Salt Whistle Bay
- **Distance:** 5nm
- Two beaches back to back separated by a narrow strip of palms
- Hike up to hilltop village — 360° views of Tobago Cays, Union Island, PSV
- Quiet in August — may nearly have the anchorage to yourself

### Day 8 — Petite St. Vincent (PSV) or Petite Martinique · Head South
- **Distance:** 8nm
- PSV: exclusive private island resort, beautiful anchorage, lunch at beach bar
- OR Petite Martinique: tiny fishing village, lobster diving, authentic Caribbean
- Afternoon: head back south toward Carriacou for the night

### Day 9 — Back to Grenada · Prickly Bay
- **Distance:** 30nm south · easy downwind run
- Stop by dinghy at Grand Anse Beach — 2 miles of white sand, finest beach on Grenada
- Final dinner: BB's Crabback in St. George's — best restaurant on the island, get the crab

## What to Bring / Prep
- Dinghy + outboard — essential
- Snorkel gear for everyone
- Paddleboards / kayaks
- Spearfishing gear (lionfish — invasive, fair game)
- Cash (Eastern Caribbean Dollars or USD) for local fishermen + markets
- Reserve Tobago Cays mooring if possible — fills up even in August on weekends

## Restaurants
- **Nimrod's** — Grenada, casual, good
- **La Boulangerie** — Grand Anse, Grenada
- **Turtleback Bar** — Hog Island, floating bar, unique
- **Lazy Turtle** — Carriacou, Tyrrel Bay
- **Bogles Round House** — Carriacou (book ahead)
- **BB's Crabback** — St. George's, Grenada, best on the island
